
PCL CE is a community-derived version of PCL developed by PCL-Community.

Create a hints.txt file in the PCL folder to customize the "Did You Know" content. Make sure the encoding is UTF-8!

In the Java Edition, you can change the language settings in the language options. Oddly enough, besides many national languages, there are also Pirate Speak, Upside-down English, Classical Chinese (Huaxia), and other strange languages.

Vietnam's Hang Sơn Đoòng cave is one of the inspirations for the lush caves biome.

Pigs in Minecraft resemble real-life Yorkshire pigs.

The white frog is based on the real-life gray tree frog (Dryophytes versicolor).

The hardness of bedrock is -1.

A compass not bound to a lodestone always points to the world spawn point, which is the first spawn point in that world.

Parrots dance around jukeboxes that are playing music discs.

Putting a glass pane on a map in a cartography table locks the map's content.

When a baby turtle grows up, it drops a scute.

Turtles return to their home beach to lay eggs, no matter how far away they are.

The number of elder guardians in an ocean monument is fixed; if you kill one, there will be one fewer elder guardian in that world.

Potions ignore armor, so don't think you can wear netherite armor to survive someone with a bunch of damage potions.

In reality, kelp is a type of algae, not an angiosperm.

The feature in Minecraft where parrots die from eating cookies containing cocoa beans is based on reality, where chocolate is highly toxic to parrots.

Minecraft is the best-selling video game in the world, with over 350 million copies sold globally, and has been officially certified by Guinness World Records.

Real-life green patina forms on metal surfaces and is quite easy to scrape off; this is reflected in the game's mechanic of removing oxidation with an axe.

The basic time unit in the game is a tick, 1 tick = 0.05 seconds, and one day = 24,000 ticks = 20 minutes.

Mojang has stated that the main reason for adding polar bears, turtles, pandas, bees, and axolotls to Minecraft is that they are endangered in reality, hoping to raise players' awareness through this.

In Java Edition, a mob named "Dinnerbone" or "Grumm" will be upside down (except for squids and glow squids).

Naming a sheep "jeb_" makes its wool cycle through colors continuously, but the wool dropped when sheared retains its original color.

Naming a vindicator "Johnny" makes it attack all nearby mobs, a reference to the film The Shining.

Although cows, chickens, and villagers have some apparent gender characteristics, according to Jeb in an interview, only the player has a gender in Minecraft; all other mobs are genderless.

Phantoms, glow squids, allays, sniffers, and armadillos were added to the game through public votes, held at MINECON Earth 2017, Minecraft Live 2020, Minecraft Live 2021, Minecraft Live 2022, and Minecraft Live 2023 respectively.

The copper golem was a mob that lost the Minecraft Live 2021 mob vote, but was later added to the game in the "Copper Age" with changed functionality.

In the article "Meet the Nitwit," the nitwit villager and the bat are described as the most "useless" mobs in Minecraft.

Mobs sometimes look at a player who is not in Spectator mode when idle.

The biomes in the Nether and End, the Deep Dark, the Pale Garden, and those with giant mushrooms are fictional; the rest of the biomes are designed entirely or mostly based on the real world.

If you apply a Nether biome in a Superflat world, only Nether mobs (ghasts, zombie piglin groups, piglin groups, and occasional magma cubes) will spawn in the Overworld, it will not rain, and Nether features and structures (such as nether quartz and glowstone clusters, and nether fortresses) will not generate, though mineshafts can still generate.

A single block can contain its own independent biome.

Specific blocks can be placed in positions where they normally cannot be placed by using special means. For example, using /setblock or a bug to place a sign in mid-air will keep it there until it is broken or receives a block update that checks whether it should be destroyed.

In Java Edition, you can use specific commands to spawn villagers with custom trades.

If the custom-traded villager is a nitwit or unemployed, those trades will work normally but won't give the villager experience points; unemployed villagers will also replace the custom trades with their corresponding profession's novice trades after claiming a job site block.

In Java Edition, the demand in supply and demand mechanics can become negative and keep decreasing indefinitely, so trades that are not used for a long time are hard to increase in price (because it takes many trades to make a large negative demand positive).

The April Fools snapshot 25w14craftmine added the "Traitor" profession, selling a variety of mining effects.

There are a total of 18 different armor trim smithing templates and 11 different trimming materials, allowing for 198 different trimmed appearances per armor piece.

Smelting a full stack (64 items) in a single furnace takes 10 minutes and 40 seconds; using multiple furnaces in parallel can reduce the total time.

Using charcoal as fuel is 4 times (×4.57) more efficient than using logs or wood, and 1 1⁄4 times more efficient than using planks.

In Bedrock Edition, using planks to craft wooden slabs as fuel is more efficient (×1.875) than using planks to smelt logs or wood into charcoal.

The ideal fuel for smelting charcoal is charcoal itself.

Compressing coal into blocks increases smelting efficiency (×1.11). 9 coals can smelt 72 items, while a block of coal can smelt 80 items.

If using wooden tools as fuel, the most cost-effective approach is to use them until they have only 1 durability left, then use them as fuel.

Although nether wood and their products cannot be directly used as fuel, they can be crafted into items such as sticks (which can also be made from Overworld wood) to achieve this indirectly.

"Smelting" is a fairly broad term in Minecraft, whereas in the real world it has a more precise definition.

Although the allay resembles a vex in appearance, it cannot pass through blocks like a vex can.

The original design of the allay, "Wisp", also appears in Minecraft Dungeons, spawning in Woodland Prisons to guide players to secret areas.

The allay's English name "allay" means "to reduce or alleviate," while the vex's name "vex" means "to annoy or worry."

The use of amethyst shards as the item to spawn allays was mainly because they are renewable, and related blocks (amethyst blocks and budding amethysts) produce musical sounds that suit the allay, also giving amethyst geodes more use.

According to an official blog post, when a player gives an item to an allay, it forms "a magical connection that prevents the allay from being accidentally hurt."

Initially, Mojang planned for allays to spawn naturally in forest biomes, but they chose to have them generate in woodland mansion prisons to highlight the theme of friendship.

Equipping an armor stand with certain blocks can cause rendering glitches. According to Searge, this is because transparent rendering for entities is not yet fully supported.

The unused lower part of the armor stand texture file contains signatures from JAPPA, Chi Wong, and Searge.

When a player places an armor stand, there is a tiny random deviation in its pose: ±5° for head pitch, ±10° for head yaw, and ±5° for body yaw. This often makes armor stands look slightly misaligned.

In the mob vote and concept art, armadillos were shown with eyes on the sides of their heads, but when initially implemented, their eyes were moved to the front because artist Jasper Boerstra thought players would struggle to form an emotional connection if the head had a blank forehead. After community feedback, the eyes were moved back to the sides.

Real armadillos tuck their heads tightly when rolling into a ball, but in-game armadillos occasionally poke their heads out when curled up, as it looks funny and cute.

The setting that armadillos eat spider eyes and scare away spiders and cave spiders is based on reality: armadillos eat a large number of invertebrates annually, including spiders.

Armadillos were erroneously described as "neutral mobs" in the update changelog.

According to Jeb, bats are a type of ambient mob — they spawn frequently, like squids, but also despawn frequently, because they serve no purpose when not near players.

In Minecraft: The Island, there is a scene where Guy accidentally hits a bat instead of a creeper at a critical moment, wasting his last arrow. According to the author, this actually happened to him while playing the game.

Camels in Minecraft are based on real-life dromedaries, which have a single hump.

The cold-weather chicken is inspired by real-life Polish chickens.

Players cannot interact with a chicken riding in a minecart because the minecart's hitbox covers the chicken's hitbox.

When a chicken is killed in mid-air, its "body" falls slower than other mobs and still shows the wing-flapping animation.

Because chickens are small, they can "hide" inside a hopper or cauldron, protecting them from lava or water above those blocks.

Notch tweeted that he would change chickens into ducks a few weeks before the end of the Beta version, causing a heated discussion on Twitter. Days later, Jeb was still receiving feedback about that message and confirmed it was just a joke.

When loading/creating/exiting a world on the legacy Console Edition, a message appears: "Is it a Chicken, or a duck?" Also, the final line of the mob descriptions reads "... and a chicken... Or is it a duck?"

The Pocket Edition 0.3.0 changelog referred to chickens as ducks.

Notch later called chickens "the chicken/duck/whatevers" in a blog post, implying that at the time, the chicken didn't have an official name.

In reality, chickens are oviparous, but in-game chickens can give birth directly to baby chickens.

In the April Fools version Java Edition 2.0, diamond and lapis lazuli chicken variants were added.

In Java Edition, chickens spawn more frequently in sparse jungles than in other biomes.

Resource files contain special animations for rusted or oxidized copper golems, but they currently use the same animations as normal copper golems.

The copper golem appeared as an easter egg in the 2022 and 2023 mob vote servers and the "Wacky Mojang Office Party" event server.

The top of the copper golem resembles a lightning rod, but it is purely decorative and has no actual function.

In officially released early concept art, the top part of the copper golem was originally designed to attract lightning and toggle the golem between idle and inactive states.

The temperate cow in the game resembles the real-life Norwegian Red cattle.

The cold-weather cow is based on real-life Highland cattle.

In the PC Gamer demo, cows had a "PCG" logo on their sides.

Notch tweeted that all "breedable" friendly animals exhibit characteristics of both sexes and can breed among themselves. This applies to cows, as they have horns and produce milk (although some real-life cow species have horns on females).

In Minecraft Earth, cows do not have three-dimensional udders.

The glow squid resembles the real-life firefly squid.

In Bedrock Edition, when a glow squid is out of water, its tentacle waving speed decreases as its oxygen depletes; when oxygen runs out, the tentacles stop moving and the glow squid starts taking damage.

In Java Edition, glow squids are not affected by the "Dinnerbone" and "Grumm" name easter eggs.

The first concept of the happy ghast came from concept art of a flying ghast mount in Minecraft Legends.

LadyAgnes stated that the happy ghast's design was inspired by hot air balloons.

Baby ghasts have gills on their appearance and use double-layered textures; adult happy ghasts lack gills and use single-layered textures. Accordingly, baby ghasts do not drown, while adult happy ghasts can drown.

In Bedrock Edition, the texture of the happy ghast's goggles is slightly stretched vertically when falling, and the position of the eyeglass frame is one pixel lower than in Java Edition.

When equipped with a harness, the happy ghast's model is slightly scaled down to keep the overall model size unchanged.

An article on minecraft.net introduced the collective noun for happy ghasts as "A chuckle of happy ghasts."

When a player jumps while riding a horse, if there is insufficient headroom, the player will clip into the block above; if that block is solid, the player takes damage.

The Simplified Chinese and Taiwanese Traditional Chinese term "哞菇" (Mōugū) is a blend of the cow's moo sound and "mushroom," similar to the English name "Mooshroom," which is a blend of "Moo" and "Mushroom."

In Minecraft Earth, there is a variant of mooshroom with buttercups on its back, called "Moobloom." In the Minecraft Live 2020 mob vote, Moobloom was eliminated in the first round with 28.3% of the vote.

There is also another variant with pink daisies, called "Moolip."

In Minecraft Dungeons, mooshrooms are hostile mobs.

"Mule" specifically refers to the offspring of a male donkey (jack) and a female horse (mare). The offspring of a female donkey (jenny) and a male horse (stallion) is called a "hinny." However, because mules are more common than hinnies, "mule" can colloquially refer to any horse-donkey hybrid.

In reality, because horses and donkeys are reproductively isolated, their hybrid offspring (mules) are generally infertile.

Four of the five parrot textures are based on real-life macaws: red – scarlet macaw; blue – hyacinth macaw; green – great green macaw; cyan – blue-and-yellow macaw. The gray parrot is based on the real-life cockatiel, with its characteristic "blush" (an orange patch of feathers over the ear area).

The parrot dancing behavior references the "Party Parrot" meme, frequently used by members of the Minecraft team in their communications.

Two splash texts warn players not to feed parrots chocolate or avocado.

Since creepers have no idle sound or footsteps, when a parrot detects a nearby creeper, it can only mimic the creeper's fuse sound.

One of the parrot's idle sounds, idle6.ogg, says the English word "hello."

If a player rides a saddled pig into a minecart, the minecart's speed increases to that of a powered rail. Whether occupied or not, the minecart only stops when hitting a block. Other entities and unpowered powered rails only reduce it to a minimum speed.

House music artist deadmau5 released songs titled "Get In The Cart, Pig" (later renamed "Fn Pig") and "Infra Turbo Pigcart Racer," referencing his experience of herding pigs into minecarts in Minecraft.

The tropical pig resembles the real-life red river hog found in African rainforests.

The killer rabbit references the Rabbit of Caerbannog from the British comedy film Monty Python and the Holy Grail, suggested by @88bully.

Although rabbits are attracted to carrots, they are not attracted to carrot-on-a-stick.

If a sheep spawner is placed via /setblock, the rotating model inside the spawner is a black sheep, but the spawned sheep will be a naturally occurring sheep color unless a specific color is added to the NBT tags. Even with a specified color, other colors may still spawn.

Sheep can eat grass through partial blocks, even when in a moving minecart.

Sheep are large enough that they cannot pass through a gap between two diagonally placed fence posts, whereas players (and most other mobs) can easily pass through.

The horse model (including skeleton horses) was designed with help from Dr. Zhark (John Olarte), author of the Mo' Creatures mod, and is based on horses from that mod. Dr. Zhark is therefore credited in the game's credits.

In multiplayer, you can determine which of two skeleton horses ridden by different players is faster: each player leashes the other's skeleton horse and they have a tug-of-war; the horse that is pulled more easily is the slower one.

In Java Edition, you can use the command /summon skeleton_horse ~ ~ ~ {Tame:1, equipment:{saddle:{id:saddle,count:1}}} to spawn a rideable skeleton horse equipped with a saddle.

Although skeleton horses are undead and cannot be fed, they still perform a head-bobbing grazing animation, presumably intentionally retained by the developers.

In Java Edition, skeleton riders do not need a saddle to control a skeleton horse, but players do. This may be due to different riding requirements, as skeletons also do not need a saddle to control spiders.

If many skeleton riders spawn in the same location, some skeletons may be knocked off their horses.

Unlike skeletons and their variants, wolves do not actively attack skeleton horses. This allows skeleton riders to be killed by untamed wolves without player intervention, leaving the skeleton horses alive.

The official English name for a baby sniffer is "Snifflet."

The sniffer's walking animation references real-life centipedes, and its sprawling, legs-splayed pose references the "sploot" position of real-life corgis.

In Bedrock Edition, attacking squids underwater causes them to rise about 1⁄3 to 1⁄2 of a block.

In Java Edition, squids have no death animation.

In Bedrock Edition, when a squid is exposed to air, its tentacle waving speed decreases as oxygen depletes; when oxygen runs out, the tentacles stop moving and the squid begins taking suffocation damage.

In reality, squids have two long tentacles and eight arms, while in-game squids more closely resemble octopuses. Real squids and octopuses have beak-like mouths, but in-game squids have teeth arranged in their mouths.

In Java Edition, squids are not affected by the "Dinnerbone" and "Grumm" name easter eggs.

The Java Edition advancement "This Boat Has Legs" is a joke about Mojang's initial plan to add "Nether boats," which eventually became striders.

Brandon Pearce once considered giving striders a freezing mechanic when they leave lava. They would curl up their legs and flop around like fish out of water until they die, but this idea was scrapped because it was "too sad."

The strider's side-to-side swaying animation while walking was an accidental development artifact that Brandon found "weird and funny," so he kept it.

The string dropped by striders upon death represents their mane, which is, in lore, the source of the string used by piglins to craft crossbows.

Baby turtles are extremely short in-game: only 0.12 blocks tall in Java Edition or 0.032 blocks tall in Bedrock Edition. This is intended to approximate the real-life size ratio of baby to adult turtles, though even then, it is much larger than real baby turtles.

Although adult turtles and baby turtles in water have no natural predators in the game, if a baby turtle is being chased and grows into an adult or enters water, the attacking mob will continue to attack, except for untamed cats, ocelots, and foxes.

The fact that turtles do not drown in water may reference reality, as aquatic turtles can briefly breathe through their cloacal skin (though they can still drown if underwater too long).

The turtle's homing behavior is based on reality: baby turtles memorize the route from their birth and return to that beach to lay eggs.

The turtle's scute shedding is based on reality: aquatic turtles shed scutes as they grow, similar to snakes shedding skin, while land turtles do not shed scutes.

The in-game goat is based on the real-life Rocky Mountain goat, and its sounds are taken from real domestic goats.

The screaming goat is not a fictional variant; it is based on the screaming behavior of some real goats.

Although a goat's ram attack deals damage, developers have stated that the ramming is meant to be mischievous play, not intentional harm.

The goat model was created using Blockbench 3D modeling software.

In the Caves & Cliffs Part I official trailer at 0:24 (the scene where a goat falls off a cliff), the constellation Capricornus (the sea-goat) appears in the night sky. "Capricornus" is also the Latin name for goat horns.

Unlike most villager-like mobs, villagers do not display a sitting-down animation when riding entities like minecarts or boats. This is intentional, and also applies to witches and wandering traders.

Villagers were inspired by shopkeepers in the game Dungeon Master 2: The Legend of Skullkeep.

Naming a villager with a name tag causes that name to appear in the trade interface title.

When villagers enter mating mode, they walk very slowly. However, when night falls or a raid begins, they will quickly run indoors.

Unlike players, villagers can sleep in beds in dimensions where bed_works is false (in vanilla, the Nether and End) without causing an explosion, making villages feasible in those dimensions. Based on this, players can deduce the Overworld's day/night cycle by observing whether villagers in such dimensions sleep.

The new villager outfits in the Village & Pillage update were inspired by 2018 fashion shows, such as Gucci.

Villagers sometimes sleep in odd positions on beds at night, hanging off the side or even clipping into walls.

In Java Edition, even while sleeping, villagers can "slide" to pick up nearby food and return to bed, which can trigger the odd sleeping poses described above.

In Bedrock Edition, although snowy taiga villages generate with snowy villagers, the building style remains the same as regular taiga villages.

In Java Edition, enabling the "Programmer Art" resource pack gives all villagers green hoods because the hooded nitwit villager texture in that pack shares the same file path (entity/villager/villager.png) as the base villager texture from before Village & Pillage.

The pre-Village & Pillage nitwit villager texture originally included a hood, but it was not rendered in earlier versions.

In Bedrock Edition, enabling the Classic Textures pack from the marketplace does not revert villager textures to the pre-texture-update version because the old villager textures are in the entity/villager directory, while the new ones are in entity/villager2.

Using commands to place an item in a villager's hand shows the same visual effect as their normal trade display, but does not make the villager offer that item for sale.

The fisher villager's outfit features a texture of the pre-Update Aquatic raw fish (cyan), intentionally retained by texture artist Jasper Boerstra.

The master-level butcher villager's trade of buying sweet berries may reference the Swedish habit of eating meat with berry jam.

Although villagers have butcher and fisher professions, based on the food they use for breeding (bread, carrots, potatoes, beetroots), villagers may be vegetarian; meat items may exist only for player trading.

In Java Edition, villagers like cats. If a cat is nearby, a villager will look down at it and sometimes try to follow it.

Villagers only actively look at players, cats, or other villagers, not at any other mob (including iron golems).

Villagers have varying fleeing speeds; they run at 2.16 blocks per second. In Bedrock Edition, the fleeing speed is 0.6 times that of Java Edition, making them easy prey for vindicators (except during raids). In Java Edition, villagers run 0.65 blocks per second faster than vindicators.

In Java Edition, naturally spawned zombie villagers with a profession become unemployed when cured, possibly because their trade data was not bound.

In Java Edition, a baby zombie villager that spawned as part of a chicken jockey will remain riding the chicken after being cured, and even when it grows into an adult villager or turns into a witch, it will not dismount. In Bedrock Edition, the baby zombie villager dismounts when cured.

Even in a boat, villagers (including babies) that have claimed a bed can sleep when near it.

Before 21w18a, the axolotl texture file included an open mouth, and concept art by Mariana Salimena showed an axolotl opening its mouth to suck or drink water. This may be an unimplemented feature.

The real-life axolotl (Mexican walking fish, six-horned dinosaur) is critically endangered in the wild. Other endangered species in the game include polar bears, turtles, pandas, and bees. The development team said adding these endangered species to Minecraft is meant to raise player awareness.

The spawn chance of blue axolotls is 1⁄1200 because 1200 is approximately the number of wild axolotls in reality.

In a development diary video, it was mentioned that tropical fish buckets can be used to feed axolotls, but tropical fish items cannot, because real Mexican axolotls only eat live food.

The axolotl's regeneration ability is based on the limb regeneration of real Mexican axolotls.

The brown axolotl variant is called "wild" in the game files because it is based on the wild-type (natural) axolotl.

The pink axolotl variant is called "leucistic" in Bedrock Edition's in-game name, and "lucy" (short for leucistic) in the game files for both editions, because it is based on the real leucistic (black-eyed white) axolotl.

Of the five axolotl color variants, only the blue one is not based on reality; it's an intentional easter egg and a reference to Mudkip from Pokémon.

Since real axolotls lack vocal cords, the in-game axolotl sounds are based on real axolotl gulping sounds when out of water, mixed with processed dog barks.

Axolotls were also called "the cutest predator" during Minecraft Live 2020, later used as an advancement name.

One of the release notes for Minecraft Launcher 2.2.25xx contained a message encrypted with the Standard Galactic Alphabet: "T⍑ᒷ ᔑ ̇/𝙹ꖎ𝙹ℸ ̣ ꖎᓭ ᔑ∷ᒷ リ𝙹ℸ ̣ ∴⍑ᔑℸ ̣ ℸ ̣ ⍑ᒷ|| ᓭᒷᒷᒲ", which decodes to "The Axolotls are not what they seem."

This is a parody of a cryptic phrase from the TV series Twin Peaks: "The owls are not what they seem."

This likely refers to the fact that axolotls look cute but are fierce predators.

A green axolotl appeared in the Minecraft Live 2020 recap video and the Caves & Cliffs Part I official trailer, but it does not exist in the game. LadyAgnes said in a livestream that she doesn't know why.

During the MINECON Earth 2018 livestream, Jeb revealed that a new cat texture would be chosen by the community. Ultimately, GoodTimesWithScar's cat "Jellie" won, and its skin was added in Java Edition 1.14 and Bedrock Edition 1.10.0. Jellie the cat passed away on January 5, 2024.

In real life, cats are famous for their ability to right themselves during a fall (though they can still be injured). This is reflected in Minecraft: cats and ocelots are completely immune to fall damage.

Before Java Edition 1.14 and Bedrock Edition 1.10.0, cats were tamed from ocelots, whereas real cats evolved from African wildcats that live in savannas.

A tabby cat named "Winslow" appeared in Minecraft: Story Mode Episode 6, "A Portal to Mystery," long before tabbies were added to the base game, and the two skins are very similar.

The tuxedo cat is based on Jeb's pet female cat "Newton," who passed away in 2014.

The white cat skin is based on the Khao Manee, a rare Thai cat breed.

The black cat skin is based on the Bombay cat, whose ancestors are the Burmese and American Shorthair.

The in-game tabby and white cats display heterochromia (two different eye colors).

You can breed two tabby cats in the game, but this is impossible in reality because nearly all calico/tabby cats are female, and the rare males are infertile.

Cat sounds were recorded from Samuel Åberg's pet cat Odi.

The tropical frog is based on the real-life gray tree frog, and the temperate frog is based on the real-life bullfrog.

Frogs were originally designed to eat fireflies to produce froglight, but the plan was scrapped after developers discovered that some firefly species are toxic to some frog species in reality.

Both frogs and axolotls are amphibians, but frogs do not take damage or die from dehydration when out of water.

In Java Edition, by modifying frog_food.json, frogs can be made to actively attack most mobs, including players and other frogs. Against armor stands and the Ender Dragon, frogs will repeatedly attempt to eat them but cannot deal damage or swallow them.

Ocelots were the first mob created by Jon Kågström, an AI expert who worked with Jeb on Minecraft. Jeb helped create the ocelot texture because Jon had difficulty with it.

In real life, cats are famous for their ability to right themselves during a fall (though they can still be injured). This is reflected in Minecraft: cats and ocelots are completely immune to fall damage.

The bee's size in Minecraft is much larger than real bees, chosen because Mojang thought it was the cutest size.

The bee's death after stinging is based on reality: a worker bee's stinger is connected to its internal organs, and after stinging, the stinger is pulled out along with the organs.

To create the dolphin sounds in the game, Mojang's lead sound designer Samuel Åberg went to Kolmården Wildlife Park to record real dolphin sounds.

If a dolphin is beached on land and in a hostile state, it will slowly move toward its target.

The white particles that appear when a dolphin leads a player to treasure are the same as those from fireworks.

In a MINECON 2017 demo shot, the dolphin's head and body were separate. In the official artwork for Java Edition 1.13, the dolphin's head and body are also separate.

Dolphins do not naturally spawn in frozen oceans. If a dolphin accidentally enters a frozen ocean, it may become trapped under ice and suffocate.

Spawning a dolphin with the command /summon dolphin ~ ~ ~ {NoAI:1b} (no AI) will cause it to survive on land and drown in water.

Before foxes were added, a model error made foxes look like boxy objects.

Although foxes appear to hold items in their mouths, they actually hold them in their main hand. This means you can use /item replace (Java Edition) or /replaceitem (Bedrock Edition) to change the item a fox is carrying.

In reality, some species of foxes jump surprisingly high to catch their prey, a behavior also reflected in Minecraft foxes.

The fox's behavior of jumping and sinking into snow is based on reality. Red foxes and arctic foxes will jump high to dive into the snow to hunt prey such as lemmings or rodents.

The splash text "In case it isn't obvious, foxes aren't players" was added due to MC-166292.

In-game fox sounds are a mix of cat and dog sounds, rather than actual fox sounds.

The red fox in the game is based on the real red fox, and the white fox is based on the real arctic fox, which in reality are two completely different species.

The iron golem's behavior of giving poppies to baby villagers pays homage to the ancient robot in Hayao Miyazaki's film Castle in the Sky. The baby villager notices the poppy in the iron golem's hand, slowly approaches, and eventually accepts it.

Iron golems do not voluntarily enter boats or minecarts. In Bedrock Edition, pushing a minecart on rails into an iron golem will cause the iron golem to enter the minecart.

The iron golem was a purchasable item on the Xbox 360 Marketplace.

The green and purple carpet patterns on llamas have creeper and enderman designs, respectively. A llama wearing a gray carpet has a mask similar to a superhero's.

To create the panda sounds in the game, Mojang's lead sound designer Samuel Åberg traveled to Guangzhou to record giant panda sounds.

The baby panda sneeze sound in the game is a mix of real baby panda and cat sounds, plus a parrot mimicking a sneeze.

The adult panda's startled reaction to a baby panda sneezing likely references a real-life video of a baby panda sneezing while its mother was eating.

The brown panda is a real subspecies of giant panda, known as the Qinling panda.

The bamboo and cake that pandas hold while eating are actually in their main hand slot. Using commands (/item in JE or /replaceitem in BE) to replace them with another item will cause the panda to try to eat that item. In Java Edition, the panda will drop the item after a short while; in Bedrock Edition, it will only stop eating and drop the item after taking damage.

On Bedrock Edition touch devices, the "Feed" button does not appear when holding a cake near a panda.

Adding polar bears to Minecraft was Jeb's wife's suggestion, as she loves polar bears. Polar bears were added after the birth of their son, Björn (Swedish for "bear"), because Jeb had said he would add polar bears after they had their "own little bear."

Unlike most baby mobs of breedable animals, baby polar bear sounds are not simply sped-up versions of the adult sounds, but rather unique. This is also reflected in subtitles.

In Bedrock Edition, after trader llamas became a separate mob, enabling the "Classic Textures" resource pack from the marketplace causes their texture to become invisible in-game. Trader llamas that were already spawned as llamas before the change are unaffected.

Wolves can teleport onto blocks with incomplete collision boxes.

In Java Edition, if a player attacks a wolf and then moves a large distance (e.g., 70 blocks), the wolf will remain angry but will no longer attack the player.

Although difficult to hit, wolves will still attempt to chase flying mobs like bats and phantoms.

Ocean monuments are built entirely from full blocks because waterlogging mechanics did not exist when they were added.

In Java Edition, if lava seas are enabled in a customized world, ocean monuments can naturally generate in lava seas; the water that generates with the monument will turn into obsidian upon block updates, encasing the monument.

Icebergs can generate above ocean monuments, causing elder guardians to suffocate.

Guardians spawned by ocean monuments actively attack squids. As a result, ink sacs from dead squids are often found on the ocean surface above monuments.

In Bedrock Edition, underwater ruins generate at roughly equal spacing in the X and Z directions.

In Bedrock Edition, a water source block always generates above the chest; it is unclear if this is intentional.

In Java Edition, shipwrecks spanning chunk boundaries may generate with different wood types on each side, and sometimes misalignment (e.g., differing heights) occurs. Old villages had similar issues.

Bastion remnants and nearby nether fortresses may connect, leading to an endless battle between wither skeletons and piglins.

In extremely rare cases, a nether fortress may generate with no interior rooms except for a single lava well chamber.

Sometimes two or more nether fortresses generate very close together or even interwoven, creating a larger fortress.

In the nether fortress lava well (not the lava well chamber), there is a 1-block gap in the nether bricks, allowing lava to flow out.

Nether fossils may represent the remains of a giant creature that once lived in soul sand valleys.

Some end city structures resemble traditional Asian pagodas, possibly referencing the Summer Palace that Jeb visited before 15w31a was released.

Due to world generation structure mechanics, the structure generation point and farthest fragment cannot exceed 8 chunks, so there is a small chance an end ship generates only partially, potentially causing the elytra or dragon head to be missing.

According to Jeb, initially they wanted a village population growth system that adapted to player expansion, but they found that identifying houses required heavy computation and slowed the game, so they settled on a simplified system where a door that "separates interior from exterior" counts as a house. After the Village & Pillage update, the definition of a "house" was changed to a valid bed.

Before Village & Pillage, village houses and farms were filled underneath with cobblestone and dirt respectively to avoid floating. If a village generated over a canyon, this could create large cobblestone or dirt pillars in the canyon.

After Village & Pillage, village buildings generate with a roughly rectangular platform of blocks below them (block type depends on nearby terrain). This often makes the terrain look unnatural in rugged landscapes.

In rare cases, you can find a village with just a single house or even just one job site (a building with a job site block).

The new plains village closely resembles the pre-Village & Pillage village, especially retaining the old church and blacksmith designs.

In rare cases, underwater ruins can generate within a village, causing the drowneds that spawn with the ruins to attack villagers.

There is space between the interior ceiling and the wooden roof of a blacksmith's house, allowing mobs to spawn inside.

Snowy villages contain a small house very similar to an igloo, but without carpet, crafting table, furnace, or basement. An actual igloo may also generate in a village, easily overlooked. During a raid, a surviving cleric villager in the igloo basement could potentially cause the raid to fail.

Villages do not have jungle or swamp biome variants, possibly because vines and seagrass would interfere with villager pathfinding.

Although villages do not naturally generate in jungles or swamps, villagers with jungle or swamp appearance can still spawn naturally when a village generates on the border of such biomes.

It is possible for a village to generate on the border of two or more biomes but only use the building style of one.

The original inhabitants of villages were pigmen, whose later design concepts were used for piglins.
